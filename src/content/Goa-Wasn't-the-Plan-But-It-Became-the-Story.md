What actually makes a trip successful?

Is it the fun, the journey, or the memories you bring back?

I'm not sure about everyone else, but for me, this trip was special it was my first trip with my two childhood friends. And that alone already made it different.

It all started randomly. We were just chilling one day, doing nothing serious, when suddenly someone said, _"Let's go on an international trip."_ And just like that, we started planning it.

Reality hit fast.

No passports. No budget.

So yeah… Goa it was.

---

## Day 1 – The Journey Begins

On 10th November, we booked our train tickets Madgaon Jan Shatabdi. We caught the morning train from Dadar to Thivim.

We reached Thivim around 2:30 PM and took an auto to our hotel, Valentine's Retreat. The place was decent free Wi-Fi, breakfast included, a pool, and all the basic stuff we needed.

But the real mission started after check-in: getting a scooty.

Out of the three of us, only one actually had a license. But with a bit of jugaad and some _"creative editing,"_ we somehow managed to rent two Fascino scooties.

Not proud. Not ashamed either. Just part of the story.

We kept Day 1 simple beachside dinner, hookah, drinks, and Goa-style biryani at Candolim Beach. The day went by fast, and honestly, we didn't do much but it felt right.

## Day 2 – Beaches, Forts, and Firsts

This was the day we actually started exploring.

We covered:

- Fort Aguada
- Aguada Rocky Beach
- Baga Beach
- Calangute Beach
- Anjuna Beach

Lunch was at Burger Factory, where I tried pork for the first time. Not life-changing, but definitely something new.

We tried going to Chapora Fort, but it was closed by the time we reached bad timing.

The day ended on a high note though. We went for a boat ride that took us into the middle of the ocean. Just us, the water, and the coastline view it was calm and chaotic at the same time.

![Burger Factory food](https://res.cloudinary.com/dwdt1dkn5/image/upload/q_auto/f_auto/v1775974352/IMG-20251114-WA0243_vfr2pw.jpg)

## Day 3 – Panaji, Drama, and Nightlife

Day 3 started in Panaji, exploring the famous colourful houses. From there, we went to Dona Paula Beach the place where Singham was shot.

Yeah… the Ajay Devgn scene.

But honestly? It looks nothing like the movie now. Everything has changed.

We kept hopping beaches after that, just riding around with no strict plan.

Then came some drama confusion, arguments, going back and forth… typical group trip stuff.

But the night? That was the main event.

We went to Bobby's Wines you've probably seen him on Instagram with his crazy drink combinations. Then we hit the famous Pink Club at Tito's Lane.

That night was loud, messy, and exactly what we came for.

---

## Day 4 – South Goa (Peak of the Trip)

This was easily the best day of the entire trip.

South Goa is just different. Cleaner, quieter, and insanely beautiful.

First stop: Baina Beach got some of my best photos there.

Then **Cabo de Rama Beach**.

Hands down, the best beach in all of Goa.

We did kayaking there and spent a lot of time just taking in the view. No rush, no noise — just peace.

By the time we left, we were exhausted. Sand stuck everywhere, barefoot, too tired to even care.

On the way back, we stopped in Panaji to buy cashew alcohol (still don't remember the exact name).

We reached the hotel around 1 AM, had dinner, and just crashed.

![Cabo de Rama Beach](https://res.cloudinary.com/dwdt1dkn5/image/upload/q_auto/f_auto/v1775974415/IMG20251116182247_bvcrc3.jpg)

## Day 5 – The End

Last day.

We did some quick shopping in the morning and then headed to Thivim station.

Our train was late by an hour no surprise there.

We reached Dadar around 1:30 AM… exactly when Mumbai locals stop running.

Perfect timing.

Had to book a cab to get home.

And just like that it ended.

## The Honest Truth About Goa

Let's not romanticize it too much.

**What I liked:**

- The beaches. That's it.

**What I didn't like:**

- Some locals were rude
- Scammers everywhere trying to overcharge
- Food was average at most places

Would I go to Goa again? Probably not.

---

## Was It Worth It?

**100%.**

Because the trip wasn't really about Goa.

It was about us.

Not everyone gets to pull off a trip like this with their childhood friends in their 20s. And years from now, when I look back at the photos and all the stupid things we did I know one thing for sure:

_I'll smile._

And that's what makes it a successful trip.
